Autism Awareness

What is Autism?

  • Definition: Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects communication, behavior, and social interaction.
  • Why "Spectrum"? The term "spectrum" refers to the wide range of symptoms and abilities seen in individuals with autism.
  • Common Traits: People with autism may face challenges in understanding social cues, prefer routines, and engage in repetitive actions.
  • Variability: Some individuals are nonverbal, while others have advanced verbal and cognitive skills.
  • Perspective: Autism is not a disease — it's a different way of experiencing the world, often accompanied by unique strengths.
  • Importance of Support: Early intervention and personalized support can help individuals with ASD lead fulfilling lives.

Early Signs of Autism

  • Delayed Speech: A child may not babble, speak, or develop language at the expected age.
  • Limited Eye Contact: Reduced or inconsistent eye contact during interactions.
  • Repetitive Behaviors: Engaging in repeated actions such as hand-flapping, rocking, or lining up toys.
  • Lack of Social Interest: May not respond to their name, show interest in others, or engage in pretend play.
  • Sensory Sensitivities: May be unusually sensitive or unresponsive to sounds, lights, textures, or touch.
  • Resistance to Change: Prefers routines and may get upset by small changes in schedule or environment.
